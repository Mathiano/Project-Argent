# Violet City — design (the first gym city, the template)

**Status:** the creative design for Violet — Argent's first gym city, built from Mathias's seeds. It's also the **reusable city template's first instance** (the skeleton extracts from here). Style/tileset per `art-reference/` (the warm HGSS-descended look); Violet uses it with its *own* buildings + an organic layout — stylistically Hearthwick's sibling, not its copy. Cross-ref `main-story.md` §6, `concord-seed-ch1.md` (the kiosk, already placed), `falkner-boss-card-v2.md`.

## Identity
**A proud, defiant, but *relaxed* highland town — modern, yet grown up around an ancient Academy.** Sky and wind country. It still trains the old way and feels no urgency about it: the Concord set up a booth in the square; the locals waved and went back to it. Laid-back pride, not anxiety. *(The "fight for the fading worth" weight stays under the surface — present in the stone and the old man's stories, never stated. It lands when the story arrives.)*

## The Academy — the heart (the key building)
The town's oldest structure, **older than the name "Violet."** The city accreted *around* it over centuries — the Academy is the ancient core; the modern town is the newer rings. Architecturally: weathered ancient stone — a wind-worn tower, an old bell, sky/wind motifs carved deep — wrapped in a maintained, living setting. **Ancient bones, modern life;** a school, not a ruin.
- **What it is:** where the craft of training is still *taught* — the living answer to the Concord's "skip the decade." Falkner is its product; the gym is the test the Academy prepares you for. **Academy teaches; Gym tests.**
- **Grounds you can enter:** a training yard where students drill with their mons; the old bell (ring it — a line about the wind); the ancient core to step into.

## The town square + the statue (the signature moment)
The city's heart sits before the Academy — round a corner and you *discover* the ancient school and its monument at once. The **statue** is the founding pair: Violet's first master and their gust-dancer, caught mid-launch into the wind. Examine it → the founding lore — the bond between that first master and their mon, the origin of the whole tradition. *(The bond thesis, quietly in stone, for those who look.)*
- The **Concord kiosk** stands in this same square — the clean modern booth a few steps from the ancient statue. The old way and the shortcut, side by side, no comment needed.

## The people
- **The old man (Academy keeper)** — sits near the statue, the lore-keeper: *"This Academy's older than the cobblestones, older than the name. The town grew up around it, not the other way round. Folks forget that."* Proud, warm, unhurried.
- **The gust-dancer kid (Gale mons)** — watching the thermals: *"See the gust-dancers riding the updraft this morning? One day I'll read the wind like Falkner does."* Aspirational; foreshadows the gym + the GALE ecosystem.
- **The unbothered local (the town's vibe)** — *"Concord folks set up their booth. We waved. Then we went back to it the way we always have. No hurry."* The relaxed defiance in one line.

## Layout (organic, lived-in — not mirrored/basic)
A **highland town that *winds*** — no symmetric grid. The Academy + square anchor a slightly-elevated center you climb toward (the focal point); the Center, Mart, and Falkner's gym sit organically off winding paths; **2–3 more houses** than the sparse first pass, at varied angles, so it reads as a place that grew over time. A **lookout** at the town's edge overlooks the valley and the route below — the highland payoff, and a quiet spot.

## Living-world touches (the free additions — make it breathe)
- **Sky/wind life:** banners and wind-vanes that turn, wind-chimes, kids flying kites in the square. Gust-dancers (FLITPECK) wheeling over the rooftops — and an NPC who feeds them.
- **Academy life:** students crossing to class, a pair sparring in the yard, a gym-challenge flyer on the notice board — next to a Concord flyer nobody's reading.
- **Interactions:** the statue (founding lore), the Academy bell, the lookout (the wind and the valley), the notice board (town news + the ignored Concord flyer), a wind-vane (*"wind's always been good here"*).
- **Quiet life:** an old couple on a bench by the lookout, a shopkeeper with local flavor, an Academy student who'll spar you if asked.

## What extracts as the reusable city template
Violet defines the skeleton every later city stamps:
- **civic identity** — one line + a register
- **anchors** — Center, Mart, Gym, + one *city-special institution* (here: the Academy)
- **a central landmark + signature reveal** (here: the square + statue)
- **the Concord beat** — its relationship to the faction
- **~3 themed NPCs** — each saying something about the town *and* the world
- **an organic, grown-over-time layout** — never mirrored
- **the biome skin** — here, sky/wind highland

Swap the skin and the specifics; keep the bones.
