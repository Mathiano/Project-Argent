# Bond Track v0.1 — what levels mean after the kit is full (P1)

The answer to "why raise a mon to 100" in a game where stats barely matter and reads win. Past the learnset, levels stop buying power and start buying **sync** — the anime promise (veteran partner reads the battle *with* you) made mechanical. Pure read-economy: it deepens tactics, never inflates stats. Slots beside Temperaments in the economy.

## Principle

- Bond is **horizontal**: it changes the ★ Momentum / Call economy, never HP/ATK/DFN/SPD.
- Bond cannot win a matchup a read or type couldn't. Sim-gate: each tier solo shifts the archetype ladder **≤3%** (same gate as Temperaments). A maxed-bond mon vs the ladder must land inside tolerance — if a tier breaks it, the tier's numbers shrink, not the gate.
- It is **opt-in depth**: a player who ignores it is never behind on the intended (capped) curve. It rewards investment for the Gauntlet/rematch ladder and for mastery, not for clearing the campaign.

## The track (levels feed it once moves are learned, ~L40+)

Bond XP accrues from *using* a mon well — read-wins, boss clears, Calls landed (it reads the event stream we already record; no new tracking). Five tiers across the post-40 stretch to 100.

| Tier | Unlock at (approx) | Benefit (all read-economy) |
|---|---|---|
| **Synced I** | bond L1 | ★ Momentum charges at +1 on the first read-win of a battle for free (one-time per fight jumpstart) |
| **Synced II** | bond L2 | Catch Breath Call costs the same ★ but also clears Winded one step |
| **Attuned** | bond L3 | ★ Momentum cap raised 2 → 3 for this mon (more Call banking, more tactical lines) |
| **In Step** | bond L4 | this mon's signature/marquee Call costs 1 less ★ (or, if no signature, any one Call does) |
| **Bonded** | bond L5 (L100) | a **second Call slot** for this mon: hold two charges, fire two Calls per battle — the veteran partner |

## Why this is the L100 incentive

- **Not power** — every tier is a Momentum/Call lever, so it survives the no-stat-check pillar.
- **The Gauntlet door** — endgame modes cap at 100; a bonded roster is your *qualified* team where reads decide, and bond is the edge that's still pure skill.
- **Mastery / collection** — "I raised this one all the way" becomes a real mechanical identity (a 3-★ cap, two-Call veteran *plays* differently), feeding dex-as-journal and Den plaques.

## Constraints / open levers

- Bond XP rate tuned so a campaign-length raise reaches ~Tier 2–3, full Bonded is an endgame investment (keeps the campaign honest).
- Marked mons (our shinies) may start one bond tier up — minor, sim-gated.
- Interaction with Temperaments: bond benefits stack on temperament stance-economics; the combined solo shift must still be ≤3% (test the cross-product, not just each alone).
- Engine hook: a bond field on the mon + the five conditional Call/Momentum modifiers. P1 — lands when the Call system itself is built (Bugsy slice introduces leader Calls; bond rides the same Call infrastructure afterward).

## Sequencing

P1, after: (1) team battles exist, (2) the Call system is in-engine (Bugsy slice). Bond is content/tuning on top of Call infrastructure, not new core combat. Until then it stays a design doc — no scaffold, no provisional data.
