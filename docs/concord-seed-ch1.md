# Concord Seed — Chapter 1 (the faintest footprint)

**Status:** the Ch1 Concord *seed* — pure environmental set-dressing in Violet City. **This is NOT the Concord faction.** The faction (grunts, battles, collared mons, the severing labs, the plot) is **Phase 8+** per `the-concord.md` ("no Phase 7 build"). This seed is *only* the kiosk + billboard `main-story.md` §9 calls "the faintest Concord seed" — flavor the player can fully ignore. Built on existing verbs (placeable object + dialog/sign + flag-gate), like the opening's scripted scenes. Cross-ref `the-concord.md` (voice/aesthetic), `main-story.md` §3.1 & §6 (the pitch + City-1 placement), `opening-design.md` (the thesis it inverts).

## The beat (what it plants, in one line)
A clean corporate kiosk, out-of-place-polished in the warm autumnal square, selling KAMON's belief — *strength over bonds* — as a **product**. The player, fresh off the professor's "the bond is what lasts," reads a corporation offering the opposite as **salvation**. One ignorable kiosk seeds the entire antagonist arc.

## Tone (the whole craft)
**Appealing, not evil. No mustache.** The horror is the *sincerity* — it sounds *helpful*. Training is slow, dying, uncertain; the Concord offers a shortcut, warmly. The player shouldn't be told it's wrong; they should feel a faint unease that this contradicts what they were just given. Clean, modern, clinical — an *institution* with branding, dropped into a town that still does it the old way.

## Elements
**Core (the seed proper — `main-story` specifies "a kiosk, a billboard"):**
- **Concord kiosk** — Violet City town square. A polished, modern product-booth aesthetic (think clean tech-store / pharma kiosk) — deliberately *out of place* against the town's warm pixel-art. Interactable → the pitch.
- **Billboard** — a static sign near it, one slogan.

**The pitch (kiosk interaction — keep it short, a seed):**
> "Training is a beautiful tradition — and a slow, uncertain one. Years of hoping a partner learns to trust you. The risk it never does.
> The Concordance offers another way: strength on the first day, loyalty without the decade of doubt. We don't ask you to earn a bond you may never live to see — we make it *obsolete*.
> The Concordance between human and mon, perfected. **Ask us how.**"

**Billboard slogan (static):**
> *"A partner takes ten years to trust you. The Concordance takes ten minutes."*

**Optional (nice-to-have, keep minimal — the core works without these):**
- **A Concord rep NPC** at the kiosk — clean, polite, *sincere* (a true believer, not a cackler) — delivers the pitch warmly if engaged. Puts a human face on the sincerity, which lands harder than a sign. Include only if it stays one calm NPC, not a scene.
- **One Violet townsperson line** — a proud-town dismissal: *"Don't know why they bother setting that up here. We still do it the real way."* Plants Violet's old-world pride (the town that still trains, §6) *and* the faint encroachment, in one line.

## Hard scope guards (do NOT cross — this is the discipline)
- **Flavor only.** No grunts, no battles, no quest, no plot trigger, no collared/severed mons (those are Ch2+ / Phase 8+). The player can walk past and never engage.
- **No new systems.** Placeable object + dialog/sign text + a flag so any one-time line fires once. Nothing else.
- **No soft-lock.** Fully ignorable; engaging it changes nothing mechanical.
- **Original Concord branding.** The kiosk/billboard need an *original* Concord mark — clean, modern, corporate — **nothing Pokémon-adjacent or real-brand-adjacent** (the Pidgey/Pokéball guard). The name "Concord / Concordance" is canonical; the logo is a small new asset (placeholder fine now, per Phase-7 art deferral).

## The thematic plant (why one kiosk earns its place)
The opening gives you the starter as "a hope, not a gift" (bonds matter) and shows KAMON believing strength wins. This seed **institutionalizes KAMON's counter-thesis** — scales one boy's belief into a polished corporation selling it as progress. It's the antagonist's first whisper, and it's *welcome* — which is the horror `the-concord.md` asks for, made structural at the smallest possible scale.
