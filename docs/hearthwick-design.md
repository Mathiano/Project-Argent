# Hearthwick — design note (the cozy hometown)

**Status:** design LOCKED — ready for CC build. The opening hometown. Job: **a warm home worth leaving**, told through **environmental storytelling** (objects, details — not NPC exposition), grown **modestly**. Decisions locked: **KAMON is a hometown local** (his house is the one new interior); **Mom stays light** (a warm note, not a setpiece). Cross-ref: `opening-design.md` (the fixed beats this dresses), `catch-tutorial-design.md` (Larch's lesson lives here), `violet-city-design.md` (sibling area), `main-story.md` (the bond thesis this seeds), `the-concord.md` (the foil this sets up), `visual-ceiling-rse-2d.md` (the RSE palette the art uses).

## The feeling we're engineering

A home the player is a little sad to leave. The departure carries weight **because the place is loved** — and the love is built from texture, not told. No NPC says "our town is special." The player feels it from the worn paths, the kept fire, the small evidence that people and mons live here, together, in care. Cozy, but in the adult register the project targets (Witcher/Mass Effect maturity): warm with a thread of melancholy, never cutesy. Leaving home young is a real, slightly bittersweet thing. We write it that way.

## The organizing motif — the hearth

The town's name *is* its soul. **Hearth:** warmth, fire kept against the dark, the place a family and a community gather. Lean into it — literally (kept fires, warm window-light, chimney smoke) and thematically (Hearthwick is where you're *from*; the warmth at your back as you walk out).

The locked lore gives the opening a free thematic axis: the cover legendary is the **silver moon-dragon** (*argent* = silver, celestial, distant). So the opening's quiet frame is **the warm hearth of home vs. the silver moon of the journey** — you leave the fire to chase the moon. A framing thread for the departure; use as much or as little as you like. (It pays a second time later: the hearth's *authentic* warmth vs. the Concord's *manufactured* version of it.)

## The method — warmth in the margins

Environmental storytelling, three registers, layered across the town:

1. **Evidence of routine & life.** Mid-day, mid-life: laundry on a line, chimney smoke, a watering can left by a flowerbed, a half-swept porch, tools against a wall, a bench worn smooth. People were here a moment ago and will be back. The place *runs*.
2. **History & memory.** A tree with a child's height-marks notched in the bark. A faded festival lantern no one took down. A founder's marker by the kept fire. The town has a past; the player is its newest small part.
3. **The thesis whisper — people and mons, together, unforced.** Where bond-over-strength is *seeded without a word*. Hearthwick's default texture is quiet mutual care: a dish of water at a doorstep for wild mons no one owns; a mon dozing in a sunbeam, plainly family; a child and a small mon sharing a snack; and the keystone, **an elder beside their equally old mon, both weathered, lifelong companions.** That image *is* the thesis — bond as time and care, the slow real thing, the felt antithesis of the loyalty the Concord will later sell off a shelf. The player absorbs what real bond looks like, so the counterfeit rings false on contact. **We never state it. It's the air of home.**

## KAMON — the warm foil (locked: local)

KAMON is a hometown local — a likeable, driven childhood rival who took the other road. **Local upgrades the mystery rather than spending it:** you *know* KAMON; you don't yet understand what's eating him — what he's chasing so hard he'd grab a mon and run. The mystery is **motive, not identity**, and it unfolds across the whole game.

Why local is the stronger choice:
- **The theft stings because it's someone you know** — a felt betrayal, not a plot device. (And it's *why* he's at the lab: in a town this small, the starter handout is the event of the decade — every local kid is drawn there.)
- **It makes the thesis personal from fight one.** His card's **0.85 bond-factor hesitation** — his mon hesitates because the bond isn't there — now has a *root*: he grabbed it rashly, never earned it. **His impatience is literally his weakness, and the player watches it cost him every fight.** "This could've been you, if you'd chased strength instead."

He is a **warm foil — rash, not villainous.** Not the tragic-wound figure (that's the Concord leader's role); just the friend who couldn't wait. His house — the one new interior — characterizes him *environmentally* before he bolts: the driven kid (training logs, an obsession with being best) who's plainly warm at the root (an old photo of two kids and a shared mon; a long-outgrown, kept memento). You read who he is from the objects, then he runs, and it lands.

## What's there — modest additions + the detail layer

One new enterable interior (KAMON's), three outdoor beats, the detail layer, the departure threshold.

| Beat | Type | Carries |
|---|---|---|
| **Player's home** (enrich) | interior | warmth, "worth leaving," Mom (light) |
| **KAMON's house** (new) | interior | the warm-but-driven foil |
| **The Common Hearth** (new) | outdoor | the town's soul, the name made visible |
| **Elder + companion** (new) | outdoor bench | the thesis whisper + departure counterweight |
| **Pond / town edge** (new, from the target) | outdoor | coexistence, soft town boundary |
| **Detail layer** | placed objects | most of the warmth (examine-text) |
| **Departure threshold** | the Route 31 sign | leaving *lands* |

One interior beyond home + lab, three outdoor beats, the detail layer — genuinely modest. And it quietly stages the whole thesis with **two poles in one small town: the elder (authentic lifelong bond) and KAMON (bond traded for strength).**

- **Player's home (enrich):** bedroom (exists) gets personal touches + the *today's-the-day* signal (packed bag, circled calendar). Ground floor = the **hearth** (fire lit, kettle on), a table, family photos, **Mom (light)**. The one place it costs something to walk out of.
- **KAMON's house (new interior):** his room does the characterization (see keystone text). Optional light touch: a parent home, briefly — worried about how hard he pushes. Keep it primarily environmental.
- **The Common Hearth (outdoor, no interior):** a covered communal fire always kept, benches, a curled-up mon, a founder's marker. Cheap, high payoff — the town's name made visible, the warm spot passed on the way out.
- **Elder + companion (outdoor bench):** the old person and their old mon, at rest. Carries the thesis whisper and offers the quiet departure word.
- **Pond / town edge (outdoor):** town meets wild, gently — a pond, wild mons at ease, a water dish at the bank.
- **Detail layer (outdoor):** the small objects from the three registers, placed deliberately. Most of the warmth — placement + examine-text, no dialogue needed.

## The departure beat (the threshold)

The northern edge — the target image: the last bench, the **Route 31** sign, the boundary of everything-you-know. We make stepping across it *land*: bittersweet, not maudlin. A light touch is enough — the hearth-smoke at your back, the road ahead, and the sign's hand-added human note (below). The silver-moon-vs-hearth frame's single clearest moment, if you use it.

## Keystone examine-text (the lines that set the tone)

The warmth lives in this text, not in generic placeholder. These are canonical; CC matches their register for the rest of the detail layer. Tone: restrained, warm, a thread of melancholy — no saccharine.

**Elder + companion (at the bench):**
> "Forty winters, this one and I. You don't learn a creature in a hurry — you learn it the way you learn a song. A little at a time."

**Elder (if the player passes, leaving town):**
> "Off already? Good. Just — don't go so fast you miss it. The road's best taken slow."

*(The elder embodies patience and slow-earned bond — and unknowingly names KAMON's exact error. The thesis and the foil, in two lines, no preaching.)*

**KAMON's room — the central objects:**
> *Training log on the wall:* "Pages of schedules, every day filled corner to corner. The last line just reads: 'Not enough. Faster.'"
>
> *A faded photo:* "Two kids and a small mon between them, squinting in the sun. You're the one on the left."
>
> *A frayed, long-outgrown toy:* "Handmade, well-loved, far too small for him now. He kept it anyway."
>
> *(optional) Shelf of ribbons:* "Every ribbon he's ever won — and a bare hook, already waiting for the next."

*(Driven to the point of impatience — "Faster" — but warm at the root: the shared childhood, the kept memento. The mystery is posed, not answered: why so hard?)*

**The departure sign (Route 31):**
> "ROUTE 31 — Violet City, north." *Below, in smaller hand-lettering someone added later:* "Come home for the winters."

*(An official sign with a human note added — warm, bittersweet, tied to the hearth and the elder's "forty winters." Carries the goodbye so Mom doesn't have to, keeping her light.)*

**Mom (at home, light):**
> "You've got everything? ...Of course you do. Look at you."

**Thesis-whisper exemplars (tone anchors for CC's other examine-text):**
> *Doorstep water dish:* "A shallow dish of water by the step. Not for any mon in particular — just for whoever's passing."
>
> *Windowsill sleeper:* "A mon asleep in the sun on the sill, paws twitching. No one's watching it. It doesn't need watching."
