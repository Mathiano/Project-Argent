# Argent Mon Aesthetic — the creature north-star (governs ALL mon design)

**Status:** canon principle. This governs the *concept and art* of every mon in the roster. It sits alongside the mechanical pillars in `mon-design-template.md` — a mon must pass the mechanical checklist AND fit this aesthetic. Banked because mon *taste* is as load-bearing as mon *mechanics*: 200 mons that feel cohesive and cool is the difference between a world players love and one they bounce off.

## The principle: GROUNDED · CREATURE-AUTHENTIC · COOL

Every Argent mon should read as a **believable animal or mythic beast** — a real lizard, a real bird, a real predator, a real dragon, a real prehistoric thing — **elevated with edge, menace, or elegance.** They feel like creatures that could *exist* (or that belong in myth), with attitude and presence.

The range runs from **elegant-predator** (sleek, bladed, armored) to **imposing-beast** (primal, powerful, intimidating) — but always *grounded and badass*, never gimmicky.

## The reference set (the taste anchor — NOT to copy, to calibrate by)

Mathias's favorites, which define the sensibility:
- **Scizor** — sleek, bladed, armored predator. Cool through menace + elegance.
- **Lucario** — the noble martial-beast; grounded, iconic, badass.
- **Tyranitar** — the kaiju; an imposing living-disaster beast.
- **Salamence** — the brutal apex dragon; raw power + intimidation.
- **Typhlosion (& line)** — creature-real, mammalian, a believable animal elevated.
- **Eevee** — the believable little mammal (and the branch-evolver — see roster).
- **Nidoran / Nidorino (line)** — Gen-1 "real monster" attitude; looks like an actual beast.
- **Seadra** — elegant, real-seahorse-elevated, with edge.
- **Kabutops** — the prehistoric bladed predator; primal cool.
- **Magby** — characterful creature-real, not gimmicky.

The throughline: **Gen 1-2 / Sinnoh "real monster" cool.** Apex predators, mythic beasts, believable animals with edge.

## AVOID (the anti-pattern)

- **Abstract-object designs** — a mon based on an ice cream cone, a set of keys, a pile of trash, a chandelier. If the core concept is "an inanimate object that's secretly a Pokémon," it FAILS the aesthetic.
- **Overly-cute gimmickry** — mascot-cute over creature-real (some cute is fine — Eevee is cute — but cute-as-the-whole-point isn't the vibe).
- **"Ran out of ideas" later-gen whimsy** — the abstract/joke-concept direction of some recent generations.
- **Generic blandness** — a mon with no edge, attitude, or distinct silhouette.

## The test (apply to every mon concept)

> **"Would this look at home in Gen 1-2, or as a cool Sinnoh mon? Does it read as a real animal or a mythic beast with edge?"**
> If it feels like a gimmick, an object, or a mascot-cute throwaway — **redesign it.**

## How this interacts with the existing systems

- **Archetype drives shape, aesthetic drives FEEL:** a "Wall" archetype could be a Tyranitar-like imposing beast or a Forretress-like armored thing — the aesthetic says make it *cool and creature-real*, not a gimmick.
- **Legendaries lean imposing-beast** (the stance-beasts, the cover moon-dragon) — apex, mythic, awe-inducing.
- **Early critters are still creature-real** — the early rodent/bird/bug are believable little animals (Rattata/Pidgey/Caterpie were real-creature-grounded, not gimmicks), just humble ones.
- **Pseudo-legendaries are the Tyranitar/Salamence slots** — the imposing, powerful, hard-earned beasts. Design them to feel *earned and awesome*.
