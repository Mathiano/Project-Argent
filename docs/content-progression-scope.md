# PROJECT ARGENT — Content & Progression Scope v0.1

**Target:** 40+ hours to *complete* (= beat the Red-equivalent on Mt. Silver), matching Silver's true ending. Every hour comes from content and depth — zero hours from grind, backtracking, or padding. The spec already deleted those; this doc shows where the time comes from instead.

## The chapter unit (×8, ~2.5–3 h each)

Route(s) → town → side beat → story/Rocket beat (chapters 3, 6, 7) → gym trainers (each win = scout-report intel) → **Prep Phase** → Leader. Rival ambushes punctuate chapters 1, 3, 5, 7, plus Victory Road and Mt. Silver — six fights, his AI retooling against your core each time.

Fight-count discipline: ~145 route/gym trainers total across both regions (Gen 2 had ~200 filler fights). Fewer fights, every one a puzzle, every gym-trainer win feeding your scout report. Set-piece boss fights: **27** (8 Johto + 4 E4 + Champion + 8 Kanto + Red + 6 rival… minus overlap) — each averaging 12–14 rounds plus prep.

## Kanto — each leader subverts their Johto twin's lesson

| Leader | Subversion |
|---|---|
| Brock | Jasmine's armor, harder: Break-only damage, two bars |
| Misty | Weather war — rain field state contested every turn |
| Lt. Surge | Timeline attacks: paralysis shoves your chips |
| Erika | Drains your *stamina* bar, not HP — exhaustion is the loss condition |
| Janine | Inherits Koga's lying tells, at a higher rate |

**Red (Mt. Silver):** the thesis fight. ~90–95% reads, Champion information rules regardless of difficulty setting, three phases, no gimmick. Everything the game taught, nothing else.

## Pacing rules

- A wall boss every ~3 badges (Whitney, Jasmine, Karen, Red) — expected 1–2 losses each; instant-retry-into-Prep makes losses cost ~30 seconds, not 10 minutes
- No mandatory fight is winnable by stat-checking past gym 1; no mandatory fight requires leveling beyond the natural curve (level-cap mode is the default-recommended toggle)
- Side content never gates the spine; the spine never exceeds 3 h without a set-piece

## Non-goals

- No XP walls, no rare-candy economies, no daily-login content in the core path
- No filler fetch quests — every Phone 2.0 quest pays intel, items, or a fight worth having
- Kanto is not a victory lap of weaker rematches; it is the harder half
