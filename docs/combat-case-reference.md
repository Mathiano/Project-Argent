# Combat Case Reference — what SHOULD happen (playtest oracle)

**Purpose:** the round-by-round "correct outcome" for key two-step interactions, to check the game against during playtest. Cross-ref combat-enrichment-roadmap (the full design), trainer-combat-profiles (Layer 4).

## The governing rules (all cases derive from these)
- **Base triangle (single-step, HARD):** AGGRESSIVE > FLUID > GUARD > AGGRESSIVE. Fluid acts FIRST (initiative) but loses the exchange to Aggressive.
- **Flipped triangle (both two-step, SOFT):** HIDE > CHARGE > FEINT > HIDE.
- **Two-step = 2 rounds:** round 1 = wind-up (phase 1, EXPOSED), round 2 = release (payoff).
- **The pivot question for two-step-vs-single-step:** did the single-stepper play a stance that PUNISHES the wind-up (Aggressive interrupts, Fluid evade-and-capitalizes), or not (Guard doesn't punish)?
- **★ goes to whoever WON A READ.** A two-step only counts as a "read" vs another two-step.
- **Charge PIERCES Guard** (a charged release pushes through brace). **The only escape from a committed enemy Charge is a CALL** (Get Away / Hang In There), never a stance.

## CASE 1 — You CHARGE (Agg two-step); foe one-steps FLUID
- **Round 1 (your wind-up):** Fluid PUNISHES a charge wind-up (evasive foe capitalizes on your exposure). → "WIND-UP PUNISHED — you're caught charging!" You take a hit; the FLUID foe gets ★. (Small wind-up chip means you're not a total punching bag, but you lose the round.)
- **Round 2 (your release):** Your charged Aggressive releases (boosted). Foe picks anew — if Fluid again, Fluid beats Aggressive (base), but your charged hit is stronger → a contest. If not Fluid, your big hit likely lands.
- **Net / lesson:** Charging into a likely-Fluid foe is a BAD trade (punished in R1, and Fluid counters Aggression). **Charge is for piercing GUARD, not for beating Fluid.** Correct design — Charge has a counter.

## CASE 2 — You BRACE (single Guard); foe CHARGES
- **Round 1 (foe's wind-up, you Brace):** Brace does NOT punish a wind-up (it's defensive, not a punisher). Foe winds up safely. Roughly neutral; foe gets no ★ (you didn't lose a read, just played defense).
- **Round 2 (foe's release):** **CHARGE PIERCES THE BRACE.** Your Guard does NOT protect you — you take the big charged hit. This is the TRAP of bracing into a charge.
- **Net / lesson:** Bracing into a charge is a LOSING line. Against a charge: **DODGE (Fluid) or INTERRUPT the wind-up (Aggressive) in R1 — don't Brace.** If already caught, your **only escape is a CALL** (Get Away / Hang In There). This case is WHY Charge exists: the answer to a defensive opponent.

## CASE 3 — Terrain (Layer 3, not built yet — designed effect)
Terrain TILTS the odds and TELLS you what to expect; it doesn't change the core outcomes.
- **Case 1 in fog/rain** (Aggression misses): your charge is even WORSE (whiffs in fog). In open field/cliff (favors commitment): charge is BETTER.
- **Case 2 in frozen/ice** (punishes bracing): your brace into the charge is even WORSE — and you can ANTICIPATE the charge (the local trainer favors aggression on their terrain).
- **The meta-read:** a smart trainer biases toward what their terrain rewards → the terrain tells you their likely play (fog → evasion/Hide; frozen → aggression/Charge). Anticipate it.

## CASE 4 — What DAZE does + is it OP?
- **What it does:** a dazed mon takes **×1.30 damage for that round.** It's both the thrice-repeat penalty AND the payoff of a landed Feint (foe took the defensive bait → Daze → +30% on your follow-up). A damage AMPLIFIER, not a stun/skip.
- **Is Feint/Daze OP vs the other two-steps? NO — sim-confirmed.** Usage: Charge 17.9% · Hide 18.4% · Feint 18.1% (near-identical, none dominates). FeintSpam (56.5%) ≈ ChargeSpam (56.0%), both BELOW balanced play.
- **Why not OP despite ×1.30:** Feint only works vs a DEFENSIVE reaction. Vs a foe who doesn't take the bait (just attacks), Feint WHIFFS → "FEINT WHIFFED — they didn't bite" and YOU'RE exposed. So Feint is strong vs defensive, weak vs aggressive — a clean counter (don't play defensively into it). Same shape as Charge (strong vs Guard, countered by Fluid) and Hide (strong vs Charge, countered by Feint): each two-step is strong in its niche, weak outside it. That's the balanced flipped-triangle rock-paper-scissors.

## Quick-reference: the two-step niches (each strong in its lane, countered outside it)
- **CHARGE** — pierces GUARD (beats a bracer). Countered by FLUID (dodge) / interrupted by AGGRESSIVE in the wind-up.
- **HIDE** — strikes from concealment, beats CHARGE (concealed when the blow lands). Countered by FEINT.
- **FEINT** — punishes a DEFENSIVE reaction with Daze (×1.30). Whiffs vs a non-defensive foe (just attack through it), countered by AGGRESSIVE.
- **Flipped triangle (both two-step):** HIDE > CHARGE > FEINT > HIDE.
- **Base triangle (single-step):** AGGRESSIVE > FLUID > GUARD > AGGRESSIVE (Fluid acts first but loses to Agg).
- **Escape a committed enemy Charge:** CALL only (Get Away / Hang In There).
