# Combat — banked backlog (real, deferred; surfaced in the Focus playtest)

Three real items surfaced during the Focus feel-test. All deferred (not blocking the slice), captured so they're not lost.

## 1. SoulSilver-quality UI upgrade (presentation-layer goal)
The current battle/menu UI is functional Gen-2/Silver-style — spare and readable, but a prototype look. Mathias wants to level it UP toward SoulSilver (HG/SS) quality: richer panels, better framing, more polish, more "later-games" feel while keeping the Silver soul.
- WHEN: the presentation-layer phase (with art/audio/animations). Not a piecemeal fix.
- Scope TBD: battle HUD panels, menus, transitions, framing. A real visual pass.

## 2. Move-vs-stance/release mapping (a real unresolved design question)
Currently there are TWO parallel things: the MOVE list (Tackle, Pebble Shot, Rock Maul, Headbutt, etc. — seen on mon summary screens) AND the stance/release system (Aggressive/Fluid/Guard + Focus→Heavy/Feint/Hide). They don't clearly connect: a Focus→Heavy is a GENERIC "heavy attack," not "Tackle-but-heavy" or "a feinted Pebble Shot." Mathias noticed (playtest #6): "when I charge it's no longer one of the moves I selected, just a heavy attack."
- THE QUESTION: how do named MOVES relate to STANCES/RELEASES? Options to explore later: (a) moves ARE the flavor/damage-type and stance/release is the TACTICAL layer on top (a Heavy is "your move, executed aggressively+charged"); (b) moves map to stances (some moves are aggressive, some defensive); (c) moves are cosmetic flavor over the stance system; (d) something else. This affects how DISTINCT mons feel (do two mons with the same stances but different moves play differently?).
- WHEN: a real design conversation, before/with deeper combat content. Affects mon identity. Not urgent but important.

## 3. More anime-style Calls (the combat x-factor vein)
Mathias's playtest insight: "Calls is the real x-factor spicing up the model... more anime-related Calls would be fun." The Call economy (Get Away, Hang In There, Catch Breath, + the bond-gated Recover/Dodge/Full Power) is where Argent's combat gets its anime SOUL — the "dodge!", "hang in there!", "give it everything!" moments. More Calls = more of that flavor + more tactical texture.
- The playtest proved their depth: Call+Get Away to dodge an aggressive foe → they whiff → they're out of stamina → advantage you ("tired him out when he got too aggressive"). Emergent and fun.
- WHEN: ongoing combat-flavor expansion. A vein to mine — design more Calls (anime-inspired), gated by bond progression. Note: keep them within the ★/bond economy + the leak cap (each Call should be a meaningful tactical option, not clutter).

## 4. Teach the focus-tell discipline tiers in-game (onboarding gap)
Surfaced in the Falkner playtest: when the player first encountered the VAGUE tier ("GALEHAWK is gathering intently"), reaction was "what does this mean?" — the open tier teaches itself through the 2-of-3 narrowings, but the vague/opaque tiers look like "the same focus phrase, slightly different" and don't self-communicate that they're a DIFFERENT readability tier.
- The TELLS are working as designed (gym leaders ARE harder to read by design) — not a bug.
- The GAP is that players don't know "is gathering intently" = harder-to-read tier vs just stylistic variation.
- FIX (content, not system): a small in-game introduction to the tell-tier concept — an NPC line earlier in the slice ("gym leaders hide their reads better"), a dex/codex entry on combat tells, or a tutorial trainer who explicitly shows different discipline. Not a popup in the heat of battle; a quiet earlier nudge.
- WHEN: as the world's NPCs/dialogue/onboarding gets fleshed out. Bank it as a polish/onboarding item.
